# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Recruiters and technical reviewers who open the site from a LinkedIn profile or similar outbound link. They arrive with little time, want a fast read on who you are, where you have worked, and what you have built, then decide whether to open the resume or reach out.

## Product Purpose

Personal software-engineer portfolio for Krzysztof Kucharski. Success is a short visit that leaves a credible picture of experience and projects, and makes the resume (or contact via LinkedIn / GitHub) an easy next step.

## Positioning

A scannable engineer profile: real employers, real projects, one clear path to the resume.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: View my Resume (PDF)
- Secondary: LinkedIn and GitHub from the header social links
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: Software engineer with named employers and shipped projects worth opening
- Belief ladder: (1) this is a real senior engineer, (2) the employers and tech stack fit the role, (3) the projects show range beyond the day job, (4) the resume is worth downloading
- Proof on hand: work history (ING, Goldman Sachs, Samsung), public GitHub projects, resume PDF at `public/doc/resume_Krzysztof_Kucharski.pdf`

## Brand Personality

Not specified by the owner. Default for this audience: professional, direct, technical. Calm confidence over flash. Prefer clarity and hierarchy over spectacle.

## Anti-references

None specified by the owner. Keep the site feeling like a personal engineer page, not a SaaS product landing or agency pitch deck.

## Design Principles

1. **Scan in under a minute** - recruiters skim; name, role, employers, and projects must land without hunting.
2. **Credibility over decoration** - employers, tech, and project substance do the selling.
3. **One primary action** - resume is the conversion; everything else supports that decision.
4. **Preserve identity** - evolve the existing dark portfolio; do not replace it with a generic template look.
5. **Accessible by default** - content stays readable without motion; respect reduced-motion preferences.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG target specified. Practical bar: readable contrast on the dark background, keyboard-reachable links, and entrance animations that do not hide content when reduced motion is preferred or when intersection observers fail.
